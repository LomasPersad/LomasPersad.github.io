    Chapter 1: Arjun Viṣhād Yog - Lamenting the Consequences of War

    Chapter 2: Sānkhya Yog - The Yog of Analytical Knowledge

    Chapter 3: Karm Yog - The Yog of Action

    Chapter 4: Jñāna Karm Sanyās Yog - The Yog of Knowledge and the Disciplines of Action

    Chapter 5: Karm Sanyās Yog - The Yog of Renunciation

    Chapter 6: Dhyān Yog - The Yog of Meditation

    In the midst of the Battlefield of Kurukshetra, Arjuna, the valiant warrior, faces a moral crisis. Faced with the prospect of fighting his own family and teachers, Arjuna is overwhelmed by compassion and grief, setting the stage for the profound teachings that follow.

    As Arjuna seeks guidance from Lord Krishna, the Supreme Divine Personality, Krishna imparts the wisdom of analytical knowledge. He reveals the eternal nature of the soul, the impermanence of the body, and the path to selfless action, guiding Arjuna towards self-realization and the fulfillment of his duty.

    Shri Krishna emphasizes the importance of selfless action and duty, teaching Arjuna the path of Karm Yoga. Understanding the significance of performing one's prescribed duties without attachment to the outcomes, Arjuna learns the essence of righteous action and its connection to spiritual growth.

    Unraveling the mystery of divine knowledge, Shri Krishna reveals the ancient wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita, which has been passed down through the ages. He explains the concept of divine action, the importance of a realized teacher, and the significance of self-surrender in the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment.

    In this chapter, Shri Krishna elaborates on the concept of renunciation and the process of attaining liberation through the renunciation of selfish desires. He imparts the wisdom of selfless devotion and the path of Karm Sanyas Yoga, inspiring Arjuna to embrace detachment while fulfilling his duties as a warrior.

    Shri Krishna introduces the profound practice of meditation, leading Arjuna towards inner peace and self-realization. Explaining the process of Dhyana Yoga, Krishna elucidates the importance of focusing the mind and attaining union with the Divine through the art of meditation.
